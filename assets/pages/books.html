The Summoner's War Trilogy

Saithia is the perfect world. It's soil is and has always been rich and plentiful, its waters and forrests abundant with life, and for those who can sense them, arcane particles are everywhere. For the past several years, the seven clans of Saithia have been peaceful. However, The Mages Guild, a secretive order of people who can manipulate the arcane particles to perfiorm magic, are facing a serious problem; A renagade mage named Taleb.

Jace, heir to the clan of Loring is also facing his own dilemas. He's in love with his best friend, Valeria, but their relationship is forbidden because she herself is the heir to another clan. Little does he know he is also being tracked by The Mages Guild because he has recently come into magical powers of his own, which is extremely rare and dangerous for someone in their early adulthood. A trait he shares with with the runaway Taleb.

Saithia and its seven clans will soon find itself in the midst of a war that not only ravishes their flawless world but one that spans between the realms. The fate of Saithia and the other worlds will ultimately be decided by either Jace, or Taleb. Who will win The Summoners War?

Book 1: The Heirs of Loring

The first book in the trilogy follows Jace, the heir clan Loring as he is tracked down by The mages Guild and 'offered' to be taught real magic. Korivar is a man with A mysterious past, and he is the one the Guild sent to train Jace. Jace doesn't want to leave his clan, but he desperatley wants to learn more about the mysteroius art of magic. Also in the back of his mind is his love for Valeria. But that love is forbidden.

Jace's younger brother Jude, faces his own troubles. Jace has always been the perfect son, and he has always had purpose, he will become Clan Lord after their father dies, but what is Jude's calling? When his brother leaves to train with the mysterious Guild of mages he faces his future head on, along with his battle with addiction.

Valeria, heir of clan Finnick, has always dreamed of what it would be like to replace her father as clan lord when he dies, but is thrown into despair when tradgedy befalls her house and her dream is thrust into reality. Can she lead as her father once did? And what of the mysterious way her father was killed? Can she expose a plan that threatens the very existance of the clans?
